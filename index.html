Who We Are

Founding

The United States Internet Pioneers Foundation was established by 20 year internet veteran Robert Formentin in 2014 to give financial aid and assistance the people who spent their careers making the internet the what it is today

Experience

Robert launched his career in the early days of the internet at Infoseek, a leading search engine, and since then has held senior management positions at Phase2Media, 24/7 Media, Advertising.com, and Answers.com. In collaboration with iVillage in 1998, Robert helped create the first women’s content channel on Infoseek, he developed an early version of sponsored content distribution with Concert in 2003, and he created one of the first native ad units on Answers.com in 2007.

Teaming Up

Through the decades of working in the industry, Robert founded USIPF and teamed up with a group of other internet veterans to give back to the many incredible people that gave so much to make the internet what it is today.

What We Do

We’re here for you in your time of need.

 

The US Internet Pioneers Foundation provides financial aid to internet veterans who are encountering an illness, going through a transition period, or are in the middle of an emergency or catastrophic event. Skilled social workers confidentially assess their needs and financial difficulties, and financial aid is provided to support the veteran’s health and well-being when income and assets are limited.

Who Qualifies

The USIP Foundation will provide funding on an as needed basis. Applicants are required to provide verifiable employment information and must fill out a detailed statement outlining financial responsibilities. All information is maintained in the strictest confidence and will only be used to assess financial needs.

To qualify for assistance, applicants will have worked for an internet company or in an internet specific role at any one of the companies listed here at minimum for 5 consecutive years and within one year of the request for financial aid.

Qualifying companies –  IAB members. Digital Agencies. Individuals not associated with these companies but that can demonstrate an active role in the internet industry will be evaluated.
